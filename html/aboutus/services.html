Service Definition

The Systems Support Team provide front and second line support to all Edinburgh University Library staff and for all
computer hardware and software available within the University Library system.

This support includes the following components

1. Helpdesk Mon-Fri 8-5 during term time (vacation times to be announced)
2. Documentation
3. Training (in consultation with Staff Development Group)
4. Development

The Systems Team manages the following services

1. Library Housekeeping System
2. Lib-srv4 - access to office automation products and email
3. Lib-srv2 - access to networked cd-rom databases
4. Lib-srv1 - access from circulation desks and opacs to library housekeeping system and email
5. Lib-srv5 - access to Ultranet networked CD-ROM databases - via Ultranet client
6. Lib-srv6 - access to Ultranet networked CD-ROM databases - via winframe and web plugins
7. Libcd1 - access to UNIX Medline for NHS users
8. Libcd2 - access to ILLOS, Library web pages and ERL databases

The Systems Team supports the following operating systems

1. DOS
2. Windows 3.1
3. Windows 95
4. Windows NT
5. Novell 3.12
6. Novell 4.11
7. Solaris 2.5.1
8. Solaris 2.6

Liaison

The Systems Team liaises with the following University departments for support, development and
co-ordination of services.

1. EUCS Micro Facilities Team - for delivery of cd-rom network and other services in the public labs.
Co-ordinating delivery of new services to public lab users.

2. Management Information Services - for giving EUL staff access to Financial Information Systems and
Personnel systems. Also for provision of student records into Library housekeeping system.

3. EUCS support teams - for delivery of services into academic departments and departmental student
computing labs. For resolving problems with users accessing library services.

4. Procurement - for purchase of all computing equipment.

5. Network Services Division - for identification and resolution of network faults. Also for the planning and
installation of Ethernet points.

6. Facilities Management Division - for support of library housekeeping system

7. Operations Department - for co-ordination of repairs to library computer hardware

The Systems Team also liaises with external suppliers to provide support for computer hardware and software.

Development

The Systems Development Team is responsible for carrying out developments on all the above listed services. They also
have responsibility for the development of new services in line with perceived user needs and in response to
external stimuli

General

Helpdesk

Role of Helpdesk:

To provide consistent and professional support and advice for Library Staff in their daily use of PCs and
printers for library work.

To deal with faults and developmental requests as follows:

Faults - to respond, log, prioritise and track all faults communicated via e-mail, sysweb, phone, letter or in
person.

Developmental requests - to advise on their efficacy and implimentation

Prioritisation of calls:

Calls to Helpdesk are split into 4 levels:

Essential
User cannot perform any duties
Urgent
Does not stop user doing all work but will seriously affect working practice
Non-urgent
Inconvenient but can still work
Developmental
Does not affect current working practice


Time-scale

Helpdesk will in most cases expect to follow the following time-scales. The nature of some faults means they
may take longer:

Examples of Essential faults:

- Advance down
- Network down
- Not able to login
- Hardware failure excluding printers

Examples of urgent problems:

- Loss of some applications
- Loss of p-mail folders
- Unable to save work
- Printer hardware faults

Examples of Non-urgent problems:

- E-mail alerts not functioning correctly
- Screen savers nor working

Example of developmental requests:

- Additional hardware requests.
- Software updates

While all users' Helpdesk calls are considered equally, Essential faults which affect service points
will take priority over Essential faults which don't.
